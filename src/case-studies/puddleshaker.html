Introduction

Design.local is an online design community focused on helping Waterloo's non-for-profit business through design. Design.local is rebranding to Puddleshaker and this website was re-designed as a part of Design.local’s rebranding initiative. For the competition, we were given a brand guide, logo, and site architecture, and stakeholder requirements. We were asked to create a high-fidelity prototype and a site map.

Problem Space

The problem space given was that the original website did not have an efficient virtual outreach towards local designers, corporate sponsors, and community partners. However, our team believed that the problem that exists is more complex than simply what was given. We have to dive deeper into this issue and understand why this occurs.

This stems from:

1. The lack of visual, structural, and thematic differentiation between all sections of the website and the rest of the website.
2. A generic website template.

Design Goals



1.Create differentiable pages for each target user (Corporate sponsors, community partners, and designers).

2. Emphasize the back story and goals of Design.local (a.k.a Puddleshaker) as an organization.

3. Emphasize the back story and goals of Design.local (a.k.a Puddleshaker) as an organization.

Design Decisions

Per our design goals, some of our most notable design decisions were:

1. Creating different pages for each user (Corporate sponsors, community partners, and local designers) which can be accessed easily through the navigation bar.

FIGURE 2.0– NAV BAR DEMONSTRATION

Circled in red is the navigation bar, which is located at the top of every page. Creating different pages addresses our design goal of “differentiable pages of each target user” as it makes all facets of Design.local (a.k.a PuddleShaker) accessible to our different users.



2. Tell the story behind the new name PuddleShaker as a part of the landing page.

FIGURE 3.0– PUDDLESHAKER LANDING PAGE

PuddleShaker is a not-for-profit whose goal is to help other charity organizations. This is one of their core values and is illustrated beautifully through their name. Telling this story emphasizes the backstory behind the organization and fulfills our second design goal.



3. Altering the colours of the original brand guide to be more vibrant against a light background.

FIGURE 4.0– COLOUR SYSTEM CHANGES

After consulting with the Puddleshaker team and with the Ontario Accessibility guidelines, we decided to go with a brighter, more saturated colour scheme as it is more uplifting and visually appealing.

Solution

Our solution was a multi-paged website, with bright colours and an organized structure designed to optimize the experience of all users.

You can view the full prototype here.

Challenges and Learnings

One of the biggest challenges for this project was staying within the specified timeline. Typical designs take months to perfect but we only had 48 hours. To overcome this challenge we followed our site map which we created at the beginning of the competition. This helped us stay organized throughout the design process.
