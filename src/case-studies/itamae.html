User Research

I began the user research process by creating a proto-persona using myself and my own beliefs. Next, to gauge a better scope of potential user demographics other than myself, I created a user survey and shared it with my friends and family. I found that another viable user demographic is 40-60-year-old women. With this new information, I interview 2 women of that age and created a persona using the data I collected. After that, I created a simple user journey map to inform the necessary screens for the app. This will ensure a guided flow without redunant and confusing steps.

High Fidelity

The screens for this high fidelity prototype were built in Figma. They can be viewed in the figure seen earlier in the document. The most important design decision I made during this entire process was the utilization of a guided recipe screen. This screen tackles most the problems identified. It makes recipes easier to read by showing only one step at a time. It also motivates users to reach the end of the recipe by showing them the step they are on (Endowned Progress Effect).

Challenges and Learnings

One major challenge that I faced during this process was researcher bias. Since I am part of the target demographic it was tempting at times to stray from the design methodology and take shortcuts. For example, I was tempted to not continue with the Persona creation and just stop at the proto-persona. However, if I had stopped there I would not have discovered another huge user demographic of older women.
From this experience, I have learned so many new skills, from sharing and analyzing user surveys, to creating design systems, and designing a mobile app with technical constraints (because I will be building it soon!).
I also learned many useful theories and methodologies such as the TRIZ problem definition, and Endowned Progress Effect which says that artificial advancement towards a goal make users more likely to want to achieve the goal.
Finally, I learned that the goal of designing is more than just creating a beautiful interface. It is about creating proactive technology that takes initiative and guides the user to their goal.
